We design, supply, and install a comprehensive range of firefighting systems tailored to industrial, commercial, and residential needs. Our expertise covers fire hydrant systems, automatic sprinkler systems, fire alarm and detection systems, and specialized gas-based suppression systems. We ensure every installation is fully compliant with local safety regulations and international standards for maximum protection.

Regular maintenance is crucial to ensure your firefighting system functions flawlessly during an emergency. At Mr. Building Solutions, our Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMCs) include periodic inspections, testing of pumps and alarms, and servicing of all components. This proactive approach guarantees system reliability, ensures regulatory compliance, and ultimately protects your property and personnel.

Our Pre-Engineered Building (PEB) maintenance service is a thorough health check for your structure. It includes inspecting for roof leaks, checking the integrity of fasteners and bolts, cleaning gutters and drainage systems, assessing for corrosion on panels and structural members, and ensuring the stability of all additions. This preventative maintenance extends the life of your warehouse and prevents minor issues from becoming costly repairs.

Investing in regular PEB maintenance safeguards your assets and operations. It ensures structural safety, prevents water damage to stored goods, maintains the building's aesthetic and value, and helps you avoid unexpected downtime due to structural failures. Our team identifies and rectifies potential problems early, saving you significant time and money in the long run.

Mr. Building Solutions excels in custom fabrication using materials like Mild Steel (MS) and Stainless Steel (SS). We can create a wide variety of products based on your specific designs or requirements, including industrial work platforms, mezzanines, safety railings, custom machine guards, storage racks, and specialized structural components. Our team works closely with you from concept to completion.

The process is straightforward. It begins with a consultation where we discuss your needs, review your drawings, or help you develop a design. We then provide a detailed quotation and timeline. Once approved, our skilled fabricators begin work, followed by a rigorous quality inspection before the finished product is delivered or installed at your site.

Industrial electrical work involves much higher voltages, complex machinery, and specialized control systems that are not found in residential settings. Our electricians are trained to handle heavy-duty power distribution, motor control centers (MCCs), industrial automation wiring, and high-capacity electrical panels. We prioritize safety protocols and system durability to withstand demanding industrial environments.
